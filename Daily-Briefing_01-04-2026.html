📚 Today's 30-Minute Learning Focus
Day 91 · Topic: Prompt Engineering

Prompt engineering is the art of communicating with AI models to get consistently high-quality, structured outputs. In 2026, it's one of the most in-demand entry-level AI skills — and it's something you can learn, practise, and earn from immediately through platforms like DataAnnotation.tech and Outlier AI.

  • Learn the core prompting techniques: zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought, and role prompting — each has specific use cases.
  • Practise writing system prompts vs. user prompts — understanding the difference is what separates basic users from professionals.
  • Explore temperature and top-p settings: how controlling randomness shapes output quality for different task types.
  • Build a "personal prompt library" — a file of reusable prompt templates for content writing, summarisation, and data extraction. Clients and employers value this.

Free resource: Anthropic Prompt Engineering Guide · Also excellent: learnprompting.org (free, structured course)
