🎓 Today's 30-Min Learning Focus
Day 93 · Topic 4 of 9: Drone Regulations (CAA)

Today's learning focus directly supports your most urgent opportunity: getting legally cleared to fly commercially before Monday's cruise ship arrival. Understanding CAA drone law in the UK isn't just compliance — it's the knowledge that lets you confidently market professional drone services, quote accurately, and operate without risk.

  • Subcategory A1/A3 vs A2: Most recreational and light commercial flying falls in Open Category. Learn which subcategory your drone falls into — this determines where you can fly and how close to people.
  • Flyer ID vs Operator ID: Flyer ID = your personal licence (free, online theory test). Operator ID = your business registration (£12/year). Both are mandatory for any commercial flight. Get both at register.caa.co.uk.
  • No-fly zones & NOTAM checks: Invergordon port area may have temporary restrictions during ship arrivals. Use the NATS Drone Assist app or Drone Scene to check before every flight.
  • Commercial operations: Once registered, you can charge for aerial photography legally. Keep a simple flight log (date, location, aircraft, purpose) — this is industry standard and required if ever queried.
📖 Free resource: register.caa.co.uk — the theory test covers everything above and takes ~20 minutes to complete including registration. The CAA also publishes free guidance here.
