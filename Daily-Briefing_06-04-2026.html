CAA

CAA Registration — Legal Requirement for Commercial Drone Work in the UK

Before charging for any drone photography in the UK, you need both a CAA Flyer ID (free, online theory test, ~1 hour) and a CAA Operator ID (£12/year, instant registration). Together these take 1–2 hours total and are valid for up to five years. Operating commercially without them is illegal and voids insurance. Start with these four concepts today.

  • Variables & Data Types: Learn let and const, and understand strings, numbers, booleans, and arrays. Open your browser console (F12 → Console) and try declaring a few variables. Type let name = "Steve" and press Enter — you've just written JavaScript.

🛩️ Register Your Drone — CAA Flyer ID + Operator ID

Today is the first day of the cruise season — which makes it the perfect day to sort your drone registration. You cannot legally charge for drone photography in the UK without both IDs. This is 1–2 hours of admin standing between you and commercial drone work.

  1. Go to register.caa.co.uk and create a free account.
  2. Take the Flyer ID online theory test (free, multiple-choice, need 100% — you can retake immediately). Takes approximately 1 hour.
  3. Once you have your Flyer ID, register for your Operator ID — £12/year, issued instantly. This is the ID you label on your drone.
  4. Download and save both certificates — you must carry them when flying commercially.
  5. Read the CAA drone rules overview so you know where you can and can't fly near the port and residential areas.
