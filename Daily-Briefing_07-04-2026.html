📚 Today's 30-Minute Learning Focus — LinkedIn

Today's rotation topic is LinkedIn — and it's one of the most immediately impactful sessions in the whole reskilling plan. LinkedIn is not a passive CV database; it's an active professional network where remote hiring managers, recruiters, and freelance clients find candidates every day. For remote work specifically, your LinkedIn profile is often the only thing a hiring manager sees before deciding whether to reply. This 30-minute session will leave you with a measurably stronger profile.

  • Your Headline is Everything (5 minutes): Update your LinkedIn headline right now. Not "Seeking Work" or "Freelancer" — write what you do and who you help. Example: "Remote Content Creator | AI Tools & Prompt Engineering | Drone Photographer | Scottish Highlands". Include 3–4 searchable keywords. This single change can increase your search appearances within 48 hours. LinkedIn's algorithm uses your headline heavily in search ranking.
  • The About Section (10 minutes): Write a 3-paragraph About section in the first person: (1) What you do and what you're building toward. (2) Your specific skills and tools (AI, drone, photography, content, customer success). (3) A call to action — "Open to remote opportunities in AI training, content creation, and drone photography. Message me." Keep it under 300 words. Use Claude to draft it, then edit it until it sounds exactly like you.
  • Skills & Endorsements (5 minutes): LinkedIn allows up to 50 skills. Add all of these if they apply: AI Tools, Prompt Engineering, Content Creation, Drone Photography, Customer Service, Event Coordination, Cryptocurrency, Remote Work, Data Annotation, Video Production, Adobe Lightroom, DJI Drone Operations. Your top 3 skills show prominently — make them count.
  • Featured Section (10 minutes): Add your best drone photo or Highland photograph to the Featured section with a keyword-rich caption. If you don't have a published piece yet, add a PDF of your CV or a link to Shutterstock/Adobe Stock contributor page. Profiles with a Featured section get significantly more clicks than those without.
