📚 Today's Learning Focus — AI Tools & Systems

AI Tools & Systems is the most immediately employable skill area on Steve's learning plan. You don't need to build AI — you need to know which tools exist, what they do best, and how to direct them for real work outputs. This 30-minute session gives you a practical working framework you can describe in every job interview and client conversation.

  • Concept 1 — The Five Tool Categories: AI tools fall into five types: (1) Language (Claude, ChatGPT) for writing, analysis, code; (2) Image generation (Midjourney, DALL-E, Adobe Firefly) for visuals; (3) Search/research (Perplexity, You.com) for accurate current information; (4) Automation (n8n, Make, Zapier) for connecting tools and workflows; (5) Multimodal/specialist (Descript for video, ElevenLabs for voice, Runway for video generation). Map your toolbox across these five categories — knowing one tool per category makes you competent across the full AI landscape.
  • Concept 2 — Prompting is Direction, Not Searching: Most people use AI like a search engine — they type a question and accept whatever comes back. Effective AI use is more like directing a junior colleague: give context, specify format, set constraints, and iterate. Example: instead of "write a cover letter," try "Write a 3-paragraph cover letter for a Senior Video Editor role at vServices. I have 5 years of photography and drone cinematography experience. Tone: confident, specific, no fluff. End with a clear call to action." The difference in output quality is enormous.
  • Concept 3 — Chaining Tools: The real power of AI tools comes from connecting them. Example workflow for a cruise ship photography job: Claude generates the caption and keywords → Canva AI formats the image for social → n8n sends the post to Instagram, Alamy, and Twitter simultaneously → Perplexity researches the ship's specifications for accuracy. No single tool does all of this — but knowing how to chain them is a specialist skill that commands premium rates.
  • 30-Min Exercise: Pick any one of today's job listings. Use Claude to write a tailored cover letter (provide the job description + your background). Then use Perplexity to research the company in 5 minutes. Then ask Claude: "What three things would make my application stand out for this specific role?" You have just completed a full AI-assisted job application workflow — and you can describe exactly this process in your interview.
