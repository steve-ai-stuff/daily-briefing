📚 Today's Learning Focus — Drone & Regs

UK drone regulations are learnable, structured, and — once you know them — a genuine professional moat. Most hobbyist drone flyers don't know the rules properly. Most commercial operators do. That difference is worth money, opens sites, and enables you to accept paid commissions. Today's 30-minute session gets you from "I know the basics" to "I understand the framework" and starts you on the path to A2 CofC certification.

  • The Three Categories of UK Drone Law: The UK's post-Brexit drone rules (Air Navigation Order + CAA framework) divide operations into Open Category (low risk, no special authorisation needed for most hobbyist/commercial flights below 120m), Specific Category (higher risk operations requiring a PDRA Operational Authorisation or bespoke permission from the CAA), and Certified Category (manned-airspace equivalent risk, heavily regulated). For Steve's Invergordon cruise photography: Open Category A2 or A3 depending on distance from uninvolved people.
  • A1, A2, A3 Subcategories (Open Category): A1 — fly over uninvolved people with sub-250g drone (e.g. DJI Mini series). No CofC needed. A2 — fly close to (but not over) uninvolved people with C2-class drone. Requires A2 CofC exam. Allows 30m horizontal distance (or 5m in low-speed mode). A3 — fly far from people with any Open-Category drone. No exam but restricted areas only. Knowing which subcategory your drone fits — and how to check — is the core competency today.
  • DroneSafe and NOTAM Checking: Before every flight, check DroneSafe.uk for your postcode. This shows you restricted zones (aerodromes, prisons, government buildings), active NOTAMs (Notices to Airmen), and temporary flight restrictions. For Invergordon (IV18): the port area may have occasional temporary restrictions during port security events. Always check. "I check DroneSafe before every flight" is a sentence that signals professionalism to any employer or client.
  • 30-Min Exercise: (1) Go to register-drones.caa.co.uk — confirm your operator registration is current. (2) Download the CAA Drone Code and read sections 1–3. (3) Check DroneSafe for IV18 right now — screenshot what you see. This is your pre-flight check habit starting today. (4) Look up one A2 CofC training provider (e.g. Coptrz or UAV Hub) and note the exam cost (£50) and what's covered.
