📚 Today’s Learning Focus — Photography

Photography is both your most immediate income source (stock library, cruise season) and your most visible skill proof for employers. Today’s 30-minute session gives you the Lightroom editing workflow that turns raw captures into submission-ready stock images — a repeatable process for every ship call this season.

  • The 3-Step Lightroom Editing Workflow: (1) Exposure first — set Whites/Blacks sliders to establish tonal range (hold Alt to see clipping). No blown highlights on ship hull or sky. (2) Colour second — White Balance to match light conditions (~6000K overcast, ~5500K sunny). HSL panel: boost Blues (sea and sky), boost Greens (Highland hills), reduce Yellows if present. (3) Sharpness last — Detail panel: Amount 70–90, Radius 1.0. Hold Alt on Masking slider and drag right until only ship edges and horizon show white. Avoids sharpening flat sky or smooth water.
  • Alamy Submission Standards: Minimum 6 megapixels (all modern cameras exceed this). JPEG at 90% quality in Lightroom export. No watermarks, no composites. Add at least 10 keywords per image. For cruise ships: ship name, cruise line, port, country, season, "cruise ship", "ocean liner", "Scotland". Minimum 3-line caption: ship name, port, date. Upload via alamy.com/contributor.
  • Building Your Stock Library — Cruise Season Strategy: Stock income is cumulative: 100 well-keyworded images earn far more than 10 excellent ones. Each ship call = 10–20 new submissions. By end of April (6 ships) you’ll have 60–120 images. By October (95 ships) you’ll have a Highland cruise portfolio no UK stock photographer can easily replicate. Target: 50 images uploaded by end of April.
  • 30-Min Exercise: Open Lightroom (or Lightroom Mobile — free). Import 10 RAW captures from the AIDAsol shoot. Apply the 3-step workflow: exposure → colour → sharpen. Export best 5 as JPEG 90%.
