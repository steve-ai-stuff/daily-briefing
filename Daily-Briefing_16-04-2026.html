💭 Today's Thought & Tip — Data Analysis
"Data is the new oil — but raw data is like crude. It's only valuable when you refine it into something people can use. You don't need to be a data scientist. You need to be the person who asks the right questions and tells the story the numbers are trying to tell."

Data Analysis in 2026 isn't a specialist's game anymore — it's a basic fluency that employers expect from every content creator, coordinator, and AI tools user. Knowing how to read a Google Analytics report, interpret a simple spreadsheet, or visualise a trend in Tableau Public makes you dramatically more hireable than someone who can write great content but can't show what impact it had. For Steve: every photograph you sell is a data point. Every Upwork pitch is a conversion rate. Your stock library is a revenue funnel. Learning to read the numbers behind your own work makes you unstoppable in job applications.
Thursday Focus — The 3-Question Data Story Framework: Good data analysis always starts with three questions: (1) What happened? (the metric — e.g. 45 views, 3 sales, 12% conversion). (2) Why did it happen? (the context — traffic source, time of day, keyword match). (3) What should we do next? (the action — change the headline, test a new keyword, post at a different time). Every time you open Google Analytics, a spreadsheet, or a stock platform dashboard, ask all three questions. This framework is what data analyst job descriptions are actually testing.
