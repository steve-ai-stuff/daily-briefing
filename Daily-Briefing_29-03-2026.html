Today's 30-Minute Learning Focus

📊 Topic: Data Analysis Fundamentals

Data analysis is one of the most transferable and in-demand skills you can build right now — and it doesn't require a maths degree. Today's focus is understanding the core workflow: how to import data, identify patterns, and present a clear insight. Even basic competency with Google Sheets or Excel pivot tables puts you ahead of a significant proportion of job applicants for analyst-adjacent roles.

  • What is data analysis? — The process of inspecting, cleaning, and modelling data to discover useful information. Think of it as asking questions of numbers and getting answers you can act on.
  • The 5-step analysis workflow — Define the question → Collect the data → Clean the data → Analyse → Visualise and communicate. Every professional analyst follows this, regardless of tools.
  • Google Sheets basics that matter most — VLOOKUP/INDEX-MATCH for combining datasets, SUMIF/COUNTIF for conditional counting, pivot tables for summarising large tables. These three alone cover 80% of real-world analyst tasks.
  • Visualisation principles — Bar charts for comparisons, line charts for trends over time, scatter plots for relationships. The goal is always the same: make the insight obvious at a glance without the viewer needing to interpret raw numbers.
