🧑‍💻 Freelancing Platforms

Topic 9 of 9 · Week 14 · Monday

Freelancing platforms are how independent workers find clients, build a track record, and create sustainable income without relying on a single employer. For you, they're the fastest route to paid work because they eliminate the need for a CV or references — your portfolio and reviews do the talking. Understanding how to position yourself on the right platforms for your skills is genuinely one of the highest-leverage things you can do this week.

  • Upwork vs Fiverr vs Toptal: Upwork suits project-based work (content writing, data entry, customer success support) and is the best starting platform for your skill set. Fiverr is better for packaged services like "drone photography package" or "AI prompt engineering session." Toptal is invitation-only for senior tech roles — not the priority right now.
  • Your first Upwork profile: Specialise ruthlessly. "AI Content Writer & Prompt Specialist in Scottish Tourism" is infinitely stronger than "Freelancer available for various work." Add your drone background, photography portfolio, and crypto knowledge as separate service offerings.
  • How to win early without reviews: Start with lower rates than you think you're worth just for the first 3–5 contracts, then raise them once you have reviews. Send highly personalised proposals — never generic. Clients can tell instantly. Reference something specific in their job post.
  • Specialist platforms worth knowing: Contra (no commission, great for creatives), People Per Hour (UK-focused, strong for writing/content), and Drone Pilot Academy's job board (UK drone work specifically). For photography: Alamy, Shutterstock Contributor, and Adobe Stock run independently of freelance platforms.
